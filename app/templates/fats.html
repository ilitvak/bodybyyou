Healthy Fats

Thats right, we are talking Fats. Common misconception is that eating fats will cause you to gain weight in unwanted areas. I would like to put that worry to rest. Eating healthy fats correctly will not cause unwarranted weight gain, the technique is proportion and selecitng the right fats for you.

Healthy Fats

Fish Salmon, Flounder, Sole, Pollock, Crabs, Tuna High Omega-3-Fatty-Acids.
Nuts Almonds, Walnuts, Cashews, Flaxseeds Almonds (About 23 nuts = 170 calories, 15 grams fat). Walnuts (About 14 halves = 185 calories, 18 grams fat). Cashews (about 18 nuts = 165 calories, 13 grams fat).
Oils Cocunut oil, Grapeseed oil, Olive oil, Extra-Virigin Olive oil, Flaxseed oil Mainly used for cooking purposes. Make sure you review the temperature which is best for cooking to not burn the oil.
Vegetables Avacado, Spinach, Kale, Brussels sprouts Omega-3's. Plants only offer a fraction of the omega-3s that fish do.

Unhealthy Fats

You've seen these ads before. Those succulent burgers and fries are tempting no? Just remember if you have serious goals about changing your body composition, leave these bad boys at the store. Unhealthy fats cause fat build up in those glamour areas. Isn't this the reason you joined the gym? Stay away!

Unhealthy Fats

Fast-Food McDonalds, Burger-King, Wendy's, Taco-Bell, Etc If it's made fast, it's most likely bad for you. Processed foods at its finest, my recommendation is to avoid these choices and find healthier alternatives.
Nuts Peanuts, Walnuts, Pistachios Peanuts are a catch-22, they are good for you, but having to many will pack on those calories!
Oils Corn oil, Sunflower Oil, Soybean oil Depending on your goals, you should stray away from these oils and stick with the healthier options.
Sodas Coke-Cola, Pepsi, Mountain-Dew, Sprite, Fanta, etc.. These drinks may have 0g of fat, but they are Extremely high in sugar, which gets stored as FAT!
